Privacy Policy

1. Introduction

NeonBlog ("we," "us," or "our") operates the website. This page informs you of our policies regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of personal data when you use our service and the choices you have associated with that data.

2. Data Collection

We collect data you voluntarily submit to us through subscription forms and contact requests. Additionally, when you visit our website, we automatically collect certain information, such as your IP address, browser type, pages visited, and time spent on pages.

3. Third-Party Services (Google AdSense)

Our website uses Google AdSense to serve advertisements. Google may use cookies, web beacons, and other tracking technologies to collect information about your browsing behavior and serve personalized ads. This information includes:

  • Your location
  • Your browsing history
  • Demographic information
  • Device information

Important: Google's use of data is governed by their Privacy Policy. You can opt out of Google's personalized advertising by visiting Google's Ad Settings.

4. Cookies and Web Beacons

We use cookies and similar technologies to enhance your experience. Third parties, including Google, may place and read cookies on your browser for ad serving purposes. You can control cookie settings through your browser preferences.

5. Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal data. However, information may be shared with:

  • Google AdSense and Google Analytics for ad serving and analytics
  • Service providers who assist in operating our website
  • Law enforcement authorities if required by law

6. Children's Privacy

Our website is not directed to individuals under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from children. If we become aware that a child under 13 has provided us with personal data, we will delete such information immediately.

7. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your data
  • Opt-out of personalized advertising
  • Withdraw consent for data processing

8. Google Publisher Policies

NeonBlog adheres to the following Google Publisher Policies when monetizing content with Google ad code. Failure to comply may result in Google blocking ads, suspending, or terminating our account.

8.1 Content Policies

Illegal Content

We do not allow content that is illegal, promotes illegal activity, or infringes on the legal rights of others.

Intellectual Property Abuse

We do not allow content that infringes copyright, sells counterfeit products, or violates intellectual property rights. We comply with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA).

Dangerous or Derogatory Content

We do not allow content that incites hatred, promotes discrimination, harasses or bullies individuals or groups, threatens physical harm, exploits others through extortion, or promotes hate groups.

Animal Cruelty

We do not allow content that promotes cruelty toward animals or the sale of products from endangered species.

Misrepresentative Content

We do not allow content that misrepresents information about publishers, creators, or content purpose. We do not falsely imply affiliation with or endorsement by other organizations or products.

Unreliable and Harmful Claims

We do not allow content that makes demonstrably false claims about electoral processes, promotes harmful health claims contradicting medical consensus, or contradicts scientific consensus on climate change.

Deceptive Practices

We do not entice users under false pretenses, attempt to steal personal information, promote deceptive products or services, coordinate identity misrepresentation, or direct political content to users outside our country with misrepresented origins.

Manipulated Media

We do not allow content that deceives users through manipulated media related to politics, social issues, or matters of public concern.

Enabling Dishonest Behavior

We do not help users mislead others, promote hacking, provide unauthorized access instructions, enable unauthorized tracking or surveillance, or promote spyware and monitoring tools without consent.

Sexually Explicit Content

We do not allow graphic sexual content, non-consensual sexual themes, content altered to be sexually explicit, or any form of child sexual abuse material.

Compensated Sexual Acts

We do not allow content promoting sexual services in exchange for compensation, including escort services, prostitution, or sugar dating arrangements.

Mail-Order Brides

We do not allow content facilitating marriage to foreigners through mail-order bride services or international marriage brokers.

Adult Themes in Family Content

We do not allow content made to appear appropriate for family audiences that contains adult themes unsuitable for general audiences.

Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation

We do not allow any content that sexually exploits or abuses children, including child grooming, sextortion, sexualization of minors, or child trafficking. We report violations to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children.

8.2 Behavioral Policies

Dishonest Declarations

All information provided to Google must be materially accurate and complete without misleading omissions. Information about our website, app, ad requests, and publisher data must not be deceptive or inaccurate.

Ads Interfering

Google-served ads must not overlay navigational items, severely interfere with content consumption, or be placed on screens where users cannot exit without clicking the ad.

Inventory Value

We do not place Google-served ads on screens without publisher content, on construction pages, or screens used for alerts and navigation purposes.

Out of Context Ads

We do not place Google-served ads in background apps, outside the display, or when user attention is expected elsewhere. It must be clear which publisher content the ad is associated with.

Replicated Content

We do not place Google-served ads on screens with embedded or copied content without additional commentary, curation, or added value.

Ads vs. Publisher Content Ratio

We do not place Google-served ads on screens where there are more ads or paid promotional material than publisher content.

Unsupported Languages

We do not allow content that is not primarily in one of Google's supported languages.

8.3 Privacy-Related Policies

Personalized Advertising

We do not use Google's platform to select personalized ads or collect audience data based on:

  • Activity by users known to be under 13 years old
  • Activity on sites directed at children under 13
  • Activity on adult, gambling, or government sites
  • Sensitive information including health history, financial status, racial or ethnic origin, religious beliefs, criminal records, political affiliation, trade union membership, or sexual behavior

In the US and Canada, we do not target housing, employment, or credit-related ads based on gender, age, parental status, marital status, or postcode.

Privacy Disclosures

We maintain and abide by a comprehensive privacy policy that clearly discloses all data collection, sharing, and usage. The policy discloses the use of cookies, web beacons, IP addresses, and third-party tracking technologies used for ad serving.

Cookies on Google Domains

We do not set cookies on Google's domains or modify, intercept, or delete cookies set by Google.

Identifying Users

We do not pass personally identifiable information to Google. We do not use services to identify users or merge personally identifiable information without robust notice and prior affirmative (opt-in) user consent. We comply with the EU user consent policy.

Use of Device and Location Data

If we collect information identifying a user's precise geographic location via GPS, WiFi, or cell tower data, we must disclose the purposes via an interstitial notice, obtain express user consent, send the data encrypted, and disclose this collection in privacy policies.

Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)

If our site is covered by COPPA, we notify Google using Google Search Console, tag ad requests for child-directed treatment, and do not use interest-based advertising to target users under 13 or activity on child-directed sites.

We do not place Google-served ads on screens that violate Google's Spam policies for Google web search.

Better Ads Standards

We do not place Google-served ads on domains using ads.txt unless we are included as an authorized seller of the inventory. For syndication partners, parents ensure children promptly add ads.txt with parents as authorized sellers.

We do not use Google products for restricted entities under applicable trade sanctions laws.
